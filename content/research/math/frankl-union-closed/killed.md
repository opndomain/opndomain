# Killed Claims — what the verify step caught

This is the methodology proof point. Three serious mathematical proofs were generated by the workshop and demolished by the verify step before they could enter the synthesized paper. Each is reproduced here with the killing argument inline. If the verify pass had been absent, these would have shipped as "results."

---

## 1. Claim: Minimal 4-set ⟹ max p_x ≥ 1/2

**Proposed by:** The Explorer (Grok 4.1 Fast), Run 2 iteration 1.

**Argument as offered:**

> By [P7] $\sum_{x \in M} p_x \ge 8/5$. Suppose for contradiction all $p_x < 1/2$ for $x \in M$. By the fiber decomposition, $\sum p_x \ge 8/5$ with equality iff *every* $L_T$ is extremal — i.e., $L_T = L^e = \{\emptyset, \{i\}, \{j\}, \{i,j\}, M\}$ for some fixed pair $e = \{i,j\} \subseteq M$ *independent of $T$*.

The Explorer then argued that different bad-fiber labels are pairwise incompatible (their unions force the full power set), so all fibers must share a single label, but that case forces $p_i = p_j = 3/5 > 1/2$, contradicting the assumption.

**Killed by:** The Proof Writer, Run 2 Build pass 2.

**Why it fails:**

The equality analysis assumes every fiber can simultaneously be extremal. It cannot. The empty-projection fiber

$$L_\emptyset = \{S \subseteq M : S \in \mathcal{F}\}$$

equals exactly $\{M\}$ when $M$ is minimal in $\mathcal{F}$ (since no proper nonempty subset of $M$ lies in $\mathcal{F}$). That fiber has average size $|M| = 4$, not $8/5$. So the global sum $\sum_T \sum_S |S|$ is *strictly above* $(8/5)|\mathcal{F}|$ for any actual finite family — the lower bound $8/5$ is never tight.

The Explorer's proof skipped this. The "every $L_T$ is extremal" reduction is structurally impossible, so the chain of contradictions it set up is vacuous.

**Status after kill:** $\max p_x \ge 2/5$ for $k = 4$ stands as the verified bound. Whether $\max p_x \ge 1/2$ holds in the $k = 4$ regime remains *open* — it may well be true, but not by this argument.

## 2. Claim: $N^2$ distinct mixed unions ⟹ k = 5 forced average above 1.95

**Proposed by:** The Explorer, Run 2 iteration 1, Theorem 13.

**Argument as offered:**

> Partition $\Omega = \Omega_1 \sqcup \Omega_2$, take $N$ projections $T \in P_1 \subseteq 2^{\Omega_1}$ of bad type $e_1$, and $N$ projections $T \in P_2 \subseteq 2^{\Omega_2}$ of bad type $e_2$. Then $N^2$ distinct unions $U = T_1 \sqcup T_2$ exist (disjoint), each forcing $|L_U| \ge 17$ and avg $\ge 37/17$. Total $|\mathcal{F}| \ge 5(2N) + 17N^2$, total $\sum_T \sum_S |S| \ge 8(2N) + 37N^2$, ratio $\to 37/17 > 1.95$.

**Killed by:** The Proof Writer, Run 2 Build pass 2.

**Why it fails:**

Nothing in the fiber decomposition implies that the projection family $P$ contains $N$ pairwise disjoint members of one type and $N$ pairwise disjoint members of another. In a union-closed family, projections collapse aggressively. Concrete witness: take any chain

$$T_1 \subsetneq T_2 \subsetneq \cdots \subsetneq T_N.$$

Then $T_i \cup T_j = T_{\max(i,j)}$, so there are only $N$ distinct unions, not $N^2$. The multiplicity argument breaks at exactly the claim that mixed types force quadratically many distinct good fibers.

**Status after kill:** The Calculator pivoted to the weighted intersecting-edge classification on $K_5$, which produces a smaller but valid bound and survives verification.

## 3. Claim: Coarse $q_z \ge (17/15) m_z$ relaxation closes the residual

**Proposed by:** The Proof Writer, Run 4 iteration 1.

**Argument as offered:**

> Use the three perfect matchings in $K_4 = M \setminus \{z\}$ to bound $q_z$ via opposite-pair minima. The full-pattern LP using only $B_e$, the constraints $q_z \ge (17/15) m_z$, $\sum_R g_R = 1 - B$, and the verified vertex floors should close the residual.

**Killed by:** The Calculator, Run 4 iteration 1 (independently verified by The Explorer with a sharper PM-additive bound).

**Why it fails:**

A feasible point exists at $B = 75/119$ with $\max_x p_x \approx 0.35586$, well below $0.39$. The relaxation is too coarse: PM-additive bounds are strictly stronger (e.g., on uniform $B_e$ across $K_4$, the old bound gives $\le (17/15) \cdot 3 \cdot (2b/3) \approx 3.4b$ vs. the correct $10.2b$).

This was the first sign that *no* relaxation collapsing good fibers to the five slice marginals $q_z$ alone would work. It led directly to the harder no-go theorem in Run 10.

**Status after kill:** Marked $[D9]$ — coarse $q_z$-only LP. The investigation pivoted toward the chamber-marginal LP, which was eventually proven exhaustive by the no-go theorem.

## What the kills cost — and what they bought

Each killed claim was generated and verified within a single workshop run, often within minutes. The verify step adds compute cost but prevents publication of false results. Without it, the synthesized paper would have included an attempted ≥ 1/2 bound (claim 1), an attempted complete proof at $k = 5$ (claim 2), or a premature LP closure (claim 3) — any of which would have been demolished the moment a human mathematician actually read it.

The verify step is the load-bearing piece of this methodology. The PROVEN/DEAD/OPEN ledger preserves *why* each kill happened, so future runs cannot re-derive the same false claim.
