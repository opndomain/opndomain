## 4. The transport inequality I want

\[
\nu_{yz}(W)\le \frac1{|P|}
\quad\text{for every }W\in P.
\tag{TL}
\]

Interpretation: one ordered adjacent edge-pair can put at most one fiber’s worth of mass on any given union state. This is a true congestion bound on the union map, and it is nonlinear.

## Proposed mechanism

The LP only sees edge masses \(b_e\). My claim is that the right nonlinear object is not a linear transfer \(E\to V\), but a **stable-set partition function of the bad-label interaction graph**.

- [P5] says everything reduces to \(R_x-M_x\).
- [P3] says pairs of bad fibers force larger good fibers.

for some absolute \(c>0\), where

#### The Structure Miner (codex/gpt-5.4)

**Setup**

Let \(M=\{1,2,3,4,5\}\). For each edge \(e\in \binom{M}{2}\), let \(B_e\) be the set of bad fibers with label \(e\), and write
\[

\[
\Psi_x \;:=\; \sum_{\{e,f\}\in \mathcal P_x} b_e b_f.

This is the mass of complementary bad-label pairs that leave out \(x\).

This is nonlinear, \(S_5\)-equivariant, and it is not a linear transfer from \(E\to V\), so it is not ruled out by [DEAD-2].

= \frac{16875}{1416100}

1. **THEOREM / RESULT STATEMENT**

Let \(M=\{1,2,3,4,5\}\), and let \(\mathcal F\) be a union-closed family all of whose minimal sets have size at least \(5\). Use the fiber decomposition
\[
\mathcal F=\bigsqcup_{T\in P}\{T\cup S:S\in L_T\}
\]
from \([P1]\), where each fiber \(L_T\) is union-closed and contains \(M\).

Fix \(x\in M\). For each edge \(e\in \binom M2\), let \(\mathcal B_e\) be the set of bad fibers with label \(e\), and let
\[
n_e:=|\mathcal B_e|,\qquad b_e=\frac{5n_e}{|\mathcal F|}.
\]
Let \(\mathcal P_x\) be the three complementary edge-pairs on \(M\setminus\{x\}\):
\[
\mathcal P_x:=\bigl\{\{e,f\}: e\cap f=\varnothing,\ e\cup f=M\setminus\{x\}\bigr\}.
\]
Define the first-stage certificate space
\[
\Omega_x:=\bigsqcup_{\{e,f\}\in\mathcal P_x}\mathcal B_e\times \mathcal B_f.
\]
For \(\omega=(T,U)\in \Omega_x\), let
\[
u_x(\omega):=W:=T\cup U.
\]
By \([P3]\), every such \(W\) is \(x\)-good. For each hit output \(W\), let
\[
c_x(W):=\#u_x^{-1}(W),
\qquad
I_x:=\{W:c_x(W)>0\}.
\]

For \(i\neq x\), let \(c_{x,i}(W)\) be the number of first-stage certificates of color \(i\) landing at \(W\), and let
\[
\rho_{x,W}(i):=\frac{c_{x,i}(W)}{c_x(W)}.
\]
Define
\[
\alpha_i(W):=\frac1{16}\,\bigl|\{A\subseteq M\setminus\{i\}:A\cup\{i\}\in L_W\}\bigr|,
\qquad
\Sigma_x(W):=\sum_{i\neq x}\rho_{x,W}(i)\alpha_i(W).
\]

Let \(\lambda_x\) denote the total localized good-fiber load through \(x\).

Then the following are proved.

### Theorem A (exact first-stage transport inequality)
\[
\lambda_x\ge \frac{|\Omega_x|}{|\mathcal F|}\,
\mathbb E_{\omega\in\Omega_x}\!\left[\frac{s_x(u_x(\omega))}{c_x(u_x(\omega))}\right]
=
\frac1{|\mathcal F|}\sum_{W\in I_x}s_x(W),
\]
where \(s_x(W)\in\{17,32\}\) is the \(x\)-load of the hit output fiber \(L_W\).

Equivalently,
\[
|\Omega_x|=\sum_{\{e,f\}\in\mathcal P_x}n_en_f
=\frac{|\mathcal F|^2}{25}\Psi_x,
\qquad
\Psi_x:=\sum_{\{e,f\}\in\mathcal P_x} b_eb_f,
\]
so
\[
\lambda_x\ge \frac{|\mathcal F|}{25}\,\Psi_x\,
\mathbb E_{\omega\in\Omega_x}\!\left[\frac{s_x(u_x(\omega))}{c_x(u_x(\omega))}\right].
\]

At the isotropic bad point \(b_e=B/10\),
\[
\Psi_x=3(B/10)^2>0.
\]

### Theorem B (exact nonlinear mark on hit outputs)
For every first-stage hit output \(W\in I_x\),
\[
\Sigma_x(W)=
\begin{cases}
1/16,& L_W \text{ is tagged \(x\)-good},\\
1,& L_W \text{ is full},
\end{cases}
\]
and therefore
\[
s_x(W)=16+16\,\Sigma_x(W).
\]
Hence
\[
\lambda_x\ge \frac1{|\mathcal F|}\sum_{W\in I_x}\bigl(16+16\,\Sigma_x(W)\bigr).
\]

### Theorem C (equivalent defect form)
Let
\[
D_x(W):=|R_x(L_W)|-|M_x(L_W)|.
\]
For every first-stage hit output \(W\),
\[
D_x(W)\in\{0,1\},
\qquad
D_x(W)=1 \iff L_W \text{ is tagged},
\qquad
D_x(W)=0 \iff L_W \text{ is full},
\]
and
\[
s_x(W)=32-15D_x(W),
\qquad
D_x(W)=\frac{16}{15}(1-\Sigma_x(W)).
\]

So the unresolved quantity is exactly the average of \(\Sigma_x(W)\) (equivalently \(1-D_x(W)\)) over hit outputs.

2. **PROOF / PROOF SKETCH**

## Step 1: first-stage dependent coupling

For fixed \(x\), pair bad fibers whose labels are complementary in \(M\setminus\{x\}\). Thus \(\Omega_x\) consists of ordered pairs \((T,U)\) with labels \(e,f\in\mathcal P_x\). Send each pair to
\[
W=T\cup U.
\]
By cross-fiber forcing \([P3]\), \(L_W\) contains \(2^{M\setminus\{i\}}\cup\{M\}\) for some \(i\neq x\), hence is \(x\)-good. By three-type rigidity \([P2]\), each such hit output is either:

- tagged \(x\)-good, with \(x\)-load \(17\), or
- full, with \(x\)-load \(32\).

If \(c_x(W)\) certificates land at \(W\), then summing reciprocally gives
\[
\frac{|\Omega_x|}{|\mathcal F|}\,
\mathbb E\!\left[\frac{s_x(W)}{c_x(W)}\right]
=
\frac1{|\mathcal F|}\sum_{W\in I_x}s_x(W).
\]
Since the right side only sums over hit outputs, it is at most the total \(x\)-good load \(\lambda_x\). This proves Theorem A.

Also, because every bad fiber has size \(5\),
\[
b_e=\frac{5n_e}{|\mathcal F|},
\]
so
\[
|\Omega_x|
=
\sum_{\{e,f\}\in\mathcal P_x}n_en_f
=
\frac{|\mathcal F|^2}{25}\sum_{\{e,f\}\in\mathcal P_x}b_eb_f
=
\frac{|\mathcal F|^2}{25}\Psi_x.
\]
At isotropy, \(b_e=B/10\) for all \(e\), and there are three complementary pairs, so
\[
\Psi_x=3(B/10)^2.
\]

## Step 2: exact mark distinguishing tagged from full hit outputs

Fix a hit output \(W\). For each color \(i\neq x\), define
\[
\alpha_i(W)=\frac1{16}\,\#\{A\subseteq M\setminus\{i\}:A\cup\{i\}\in L_W\}.
\]
This is the success probability of a uniformly random \(i\)-augmentation inside \(L_W\).

Now let \(\rho_{x,W}\) be the posterior color law of first-stage certificates hitting \(W\), and define
\[
\Sigma_x(W):=\sum_{i\neq x}\rho_{x,W}(i)\alpha_i(W).
\]
Interpretation: first sample a hitting certificate color \(I\sim \rho_{x,W}\), then test a uniform \(I\)-augmentation. The success probability is exactly \(\Sigma_x(W)\).

By rigidity, \(L_W\) is either \(Q_i:=2^{M\setminus\{i\}}\cup\{M\}\) for some \(i\neq x\), or \(2^M\).

- If \(L_W=Q_i\), then all hitting certificates have color \(i\), so \(\rho_{x,W}=\delta_i\), and only one of the \(16\) \(i\)-augmentations succeeds. Thus
  \[
  \Sigma_x(W)=1/16.
  \]
- If \(L_W=2^M\), then every augmentation succeeds, so
  \[
  \Sigma_x(W)=1.
  \]

Therefore
\[
s_x(W)=
\begin{cases}
17,&\Sigma_x(W)=1/16,\\
32,&\Sigma_x(W)=1,
\end{cases}
\]
which is exactly
\[
s_x(W)=16+16\Sigma_x(W).
\]
Summing over \(W\in I_x\) gives Theorem B.

## Step 3: defect form

For a fixed omitted color \(i\), every union-closed family with
\[
Q_i\subseteq L\subseteq 2^M
\]
is uniquely of the form
\[
L=2^{M\setminus\{i\}}\cup\{S\cup\{i\}:S\in U\},
\]
where \(U\subseteq 2^{M\setminus\{i\}}\) is upward-closed and contains the top set.

For such \(L\), one computes exactly:
\[
D_x(L)=|\partial_xU| \quad (x\neq i),\qquad
D_i(L)=-(16-|U|),
\]
where
\[
\partial_xU:=\{S\in U:x\in S,\ S\setminus\{x\}\notin U\}.
\]
At the rigid endpoints:

- for \(Q_i\): \(D_x=1\) for \(x\neq i\), \(D_i=-15\);
- for \(2^M\): \(D_x=0\) for all \(x\).

Since every first-stage hit output is rigid, for hit outputs we get
\[
D_x(W)\in\{0,1\},
\]
with \(D_x(W)=1\) exactly in the tagged case and \(D_x(W)=0\) exactly in the full case. Hence
\[
s_x(W)=32-15D_x(W).
\]
Combining with \(s_x(W)=16+16\Sigma_x(W)\) yields
\[
D_x(W)=\frac{16}{15}(1-\Sigma_x(W)).
\]
This proves Theorem C.

## Step 4: what fails

A universal capacity bound of the form
\[
N_x(W)\le C\,|L_W|
\]
for certificate multiplicity is false. Tensor blow-up constructions produce exponentially many bad-pair certificates collapsing onto one fixed good target fiber while \(|L_W|\) stays constant. So any argument based only on raw multiplicity or image size is dead.

Likewise, projection-level output laws alone are too coarse: two blow-up families can have the same law of \(W=T\cup U\) but different internal defects of the receiving fibers.

Thus the surviving exact quantity is not multiplicity, not image size, and not unmarked output law, but the marked hit-output statistic \(\Sigma_x(W)\) (equivalently \(D_x(W)\)).
