## MECHANISMS (conserved quantities, forcing principles that survived stress testing)
- [M1] Low ballistic coefficient moves the heating optimum upward to \(\rho_=\beta\sin|\gamma|/(3H)\). That simultaneously lowers peak flux and keeps the total acreage heat load in the few 10 MJ/m² range even though the vehicle dissipates terajoules overall. (survived: The Aerothermodynamicist, iter 0)
- [M2] Acreage and edges are different problems. Acreage is reradiation-plus-diffusion limited; sharp edges are temperature-limit limited. (survived: The Aerothermodynamicist, iter 0)
- [M3] The reusable acreage floor is a diffusion-and-attachment floor, not an enthalpy floor. Once \( \epsilon \sigma T_{\max}^4 \) exceeds local peak heating, extra material is purchased to delay bondline rise, not to absorb total entry energy. (survived: The TPS Designer, iter 0)
- [M4] For reusable acreage, the decisive inequality is pointwise: (survived: The Limits Physicist, iter 0)
- [M5] Radiation and convection peak at different densities. Convective peak sits at \(\rho\propto \beta\sin|\gamma|/H\) [P1], while radiative peak sits higher, at \(\rho\propto \beta\sin|\gamma|/(nH)\) with \(n\gg 1\). That steep \(V^n\) penalty is why LEO-return radiation stays weak even when convective heating is substantial. (survived: The Aerothermodynamicist, iter 0)
- [M6] Off stagnation, radiation is crushed by the same thing that weakens the shock: only the normal component of velocity feeds the shock-layer temperature. Because radiation carries a high velocity exponent, the obliquity penalty is much harsher for radiation than for convection. (survived: The TPS Designer, iter 0)
- [M7] In local thin-layer radiation, transport is not the amplifier; local source strength is. Off-stagnation suppression is therefore controlled primarily by the shock-strength law \(V_n^n\), not by ray geometry. (survived: The Limits Physicist, iter 0)
- [M8] Off-stagnation radiative uncertainty is controlled by shock-shape curvature mismatch, not by transport amplification. Once [P29] fixes the \(\cos^n\theta\) source suppression, the only remaining lever is how rapidly the normal shock-layer thickness grows downstream. (survived: The Aerothermodynamicist, iter 0)
- [M9] Hot-structure credit changes reusable TPS sizing from “protect a cold bondline” to “meter heat into a warm structural heat sink.” Because the diffusion thickness scales with an inverse error function of the allowable interface temperature, modest relaxation of interface temperature cuts tile mass strongly. (survived: The TPS Designer, iter 0)
- [M10] Finite reradiation power creates an unavoidable transient enthalpy backlog whenever \(q_{\rm in}\) locally outruns \(\epsilon\sigma T_{\max}^4\). That backlog, not total entry energy by itself, is what forces positive TPS mass on hot spots. (survived: The Limits Physicist, iter 0)
