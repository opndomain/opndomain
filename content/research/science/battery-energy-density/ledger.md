## MECHANISMS (conserved quantities, forcing principles that survived stress testing)
- [M1] Pore-volume locking — in liquid intercalation cells, every stored Ah requires a fixed volume of wetted pore network once chemistry, porosity, and formulation are fixed; this creates a non-vanishing electrolyte mass floor even as packaging and collector overhead shrink. (survived: The Thermodynamicist, iter 0)
- [M2] Ion-path mass locking — every rechargeable cell must devote a finite mass per transferred Ah to an ion-conducting pathway. In liquid intercalation cells this appears as wetted pore volume; in Li-S it appears as catholyte inventory; in solid-state it appears as separator and composite-electrode solid electrolyte. The medium changes, but the ion-path mass does not vanish. (survived: The Cell Engineer, iter 0)
- [M3] Thickness-floor transfer — transport laws by themselves only cap \(jL\); a nonzero mass floor appears only when some independent physics forbids \(L\to 0\). The true ceiling is therefore set by whatever transfers a thickness floor into the ion path. (survived: The Limits Analyst, iter 0)
- [M4] Denominator theft — every non-redox gram steals specific energy by enlarging the mass-per-energy denominator. In fixed-voltage comparisons, the natural conserved bookkeeping variable is g/Wh, not Wh/kg. (survived: The Thermodynamicist, iter 0)
- [M5] Host-erasure leverage — high-energy gains from Li metal come mainly from deleting the anode host architecture and its support masses, not from the small intrinsic mass of lithium itself. (survived: The Cell Engineer, iter 0)
- [M6] Thickness-load coupling — increasing areal capacity requires thicker active layers, but porous-electrolyte salt transport constrains the product \(jL\); at fixed discharge time this converts directly into a finite \(q_{\max}\), which in turn leaves a positive residual denominator from all \(1/q\)-scaling architecture terms. (survived: The Limits Analyst, iter 0)
- [M7] Path relocation without path erasure — solid-state can delete liquid electrolyte, but if ionic continuity is restored by dense separator mass plus ionically percolating inactive cathode composite, the ion-path denominator is transferred rather than removed. (survived: The Thermodynamicist, iter 0)
- [M8] Catholyte domination — in soluble-polysulfide Li-S, the transport medium is not locked by pore geometry but by redox-species management; the required catholyte inventory can exceed every other denominator term and become the principal thief of specific energy. (survived: The Cell Engineer, iter 0)
- [M9] Extreme-value insulation floor — once the electronically insulating gap must survive over a huge number of statistically rough patches, separator thickness is set by an extreme-value quantile, not by mean roughness or ionic conductivity. (survived: The Limits Analyst, iter 0)
